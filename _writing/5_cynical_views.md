There must be a disenchanted view to complete the circle. After all, all views are equally right, all views are partial, all views are thus wrong. This is another wrong view. Any view that I try to put into writing is the wrong view. Because the only right view, is the non-view. When one holds no views one is liberated. 


Let us begin, shall we?

All concepts fly around, especially in the online space nowadays, and one that stands out the most
is the concept of "ego". Refered to as "self" or identity, it is such an abstract term that 
grasping what it means is very confusing. No wonder people try to make it concrete then, and 
associate the ego with the body, the mind, the experience or even the very being. 
Some assume it is the construction of identity, ideas about self, others discuss about a sense of self or feeling of self. Freud meant it one way, Buddha meant it another way, and most other people use it as a synonym for pride in everyday situations. All these concepts fly around, miscommunication abounds, people get led astray. 

There are things. Are there not? You look around and see a tree. You look around and see a bush. 

What delineates the tree from its surrounding? Where are the precise boundaries of the tree?
What delineates a tree from a bush? When is it a tree and when is it a bush? What happens when you can't tell if it's a damned tree or bloody bush? Does it have to be and either/or? Does the tree have to have limits? 

What if you can't make a clear delineation between what is the tree and its roots? Or the ground in which is is planted or the planet from which it springs and the galaxy in which it spins? Where precisely do you draw conceptual limits? You certainly don't do it consciously, because there is a little part in the brain, called the inferior temporal cortex, unconsciously and automatically doing what's called general object recognition, while the parietal cortex maps incoming visual information onto stored, familiar representations of an object, such as its size or orientation, in this case a large upward tree. And this is a known fact, a scientific finding, not some assumption like nephrology used to make. 

It is what is called a heuristic or pattern recognition. It's automatic. It can fail. It can fail entirely and then you go beyond what Huxley called "the Doors of Perception" with a bit of mescaline, or you may get a stroke and experience everything like neuroscientist and author Jill Bolte Taylor wrote in her book "My stroke of insight". Or you may do meditation a lot and one day you get there and call it Samādhi. 

That's reality unbound for you: seeing what is, not what you "think" it is. And what is ... is basically meaningless, yet is. It nevertheless keeps going on, keeps on being, quite independent of you, it posits itself upon your consciousness as long as you still have one flying around and you're not dead. Or asleep. Or unconscious. Or undergoing full body anesthesia for a complicated operation. 

This is what enlightenment is, for those who want a down to earth explanation. It is a hacking of your brain. Imagine for thousands of years in India people suffering. Too much heat, too little food, too much emotionality and passion, floods, disasters, snakes and wild animals praying on their babies, diseases and ultimately all this death. People needed anti-depressants, but there weren't any around, so they started to pay attention inward. Now, Indian people were no fools, they were very clever and some of them particularly wise. It all started really with Patanjali (it's just my view of things, yours may differ). He's the father of "yoga" and the famous Yoga Sutras. He discovered one can alter experience and hack consciousness. And that's where it all begins. 

But similar attempts were done by most other civilizations, we have esoteric traditions all around the world, from simple tribalistic animism and shamanism to complex metaphysics, occult sciences and mysticism. Nowadays we have psychology and neuroscience too. And progressive politics (which also try to hack the mind in their own way). 

But, along the path of just seeking peace and blissful joy, people inquired into the grand questions of existence: what is life, what is subjective vs objective, what is time, what is me, what exists and what is real, ... etc. They often wondered about what is the all-ness that makes up everything, and named it names, like gods and things. 

All simple, clear and logical. Anyone knows this, even those who don't study much, have an intuitive grasp about it. The wonder. The mystery. The great beyond. 

Plentiful of pathologies out there (short term memory loss, long term memory loss, traumas, repression, schizophrenia, etc.) are more iluminating onto how the human brain works than a thousand years in temples doing rituals. But it might be that the rituals work, the knowledge itself not so much. Doing the rituals may aleviate suffering, gaining the knowledge (as Kohelet rightfully wrote) increases the sorrows. 

No brain, no experience - death. Simple as that. 

Some say: "a consciousness that transcends the material, that lives on beyond". I say, where does it go when you sleep, when you go into surgery and undergo anesthesia? You die every night just to re-emerge the next day. Has anyone proven otherwise? I know what you are thinking: NDEs. Yes. Near death experiences, people going, coming back, seeing things. Well, two fascinating things worth mentioning: they all see things they "believe" according to their religions, or nothing at all. Never a muslim goes beyond and comes back a Jesus man. Nor does a Jewish woman go and comes back a Shiva worshipper. They go and see the fairytales of their childhood, or the progressive new age bullshit of their adulthood, which by the way today comes in spades (probably next to porn, new-age magic thinking is the second most spread poision on the world wide web). 

I have yet to see how relevant individual consciousness retention after death (that would be ego retention after death, the "me" being still "me") would be in the legal world. Because it would be amazing to recuperate your money from a reincarnated debtor wouldn't it? After all, they stole the money in their previous lives, didn't they? They must pay. Sue them! 

We die every night, every day we are reborn. Fear of death is irrational, is a pure emotional aspect of the "self" or "ego". You see, truth dissolves all else like acid. But some people need untruth to function. If you take it away, they not only hate you, they hate themselves. Their life gets miserable really fast.

And then there is the question of real suffering in the world. Look around, how many people live in cozy western houses, with air conditioning, central heating, artistic interior design, macbooks and wellness gimmicks all around? How many people eat healthy super foods and live young forever, have sex for pleasure and fun and literally enjoy every aspect of their lives? Not that many I assure you and not even those for too long. 

People go through tragedy all over the earth (some even say the earth is flat - sigh), they die of starvation, cancer, diseases, accidents, they degenerate and get old, they get crippled, they are tortured and maimed and raped by other humans (but some say evil doesn't exist and some say reality doesn't exist and some say existence doesn't exist), people live through loss, pain and solitude, struggle with debilitating depression and anxieties and panics that some dismiss as them imagining it. 

And worse of all, people lack love. The love to heal, to trust and to hope, to endure another day without collapsing under the weight of being alive. To love life (in others and in self) enough to transcend the selfishness of survival. To love their way into life, and by it to gain the strength and the determination to make life better for us all. That is love. Pain is not a theory and starvation is not a metaphor. A mother burying her child is not a misperception of consciousness. If spirituality cannot look directly at horror without denying it, it is not wisdom but sociopathic anesthesia. And remember, loving takes courage. It takes more courage to love than to die. It only takes cowardice to become a sociopathic selfish worm caring only about their own gratification - exclusively. Harsh words, but some must wake! 

Any philosophy that erases the reality of suffering becomes morally dangerous. Not because it is metaphysically wrong (so I'm not speaking of man made morals just to be sure), but because it tempts us to withdraw from responsibility and thus it indirectly encourages and creates the very suffering it preaches to eradicate. One has to see this, there's no way around it. To be responsible means to respond, and without responding one severs themselves from life. 

Love is something entirely foreign to most human beings. On the other hand, selfishness is the condition of most of this planet and not only is it being more encouraged and cultivated (by all the self-help books, modern psychology, spirituality and atheism alike) but praised as the ultimate path to happiness. If it wasn't with the tears of love and kindness that we look at this predicament (and become moved to address it), then it would look like nothing short of pathetic.

 
You can explain (or try hard to explain) the suffering of a 12 years old girl who got tortured and killed by a serial killer, but you cannot take that pain away. You cannot fix it. And in trying to explain it (and believe me, serious scholars, rabbis and philosophers, priests and gurus have tried each in their own schools of thought and with best intentions), you only end up siding with the criminal even if partially, because you need to explain some necessity for why it happened. That's not just psychopathic, not only cruel, that is downright destructive and even nihilistic I might add. 

And the psychopath is again a most interesting example of human being. Yes, they walk among us even if this seems so hard to imagine because they don't prowl with a big sign on their foreheads. Unless they become "spiritual leaders" (which they often do, and then teach people illumination - imagine that), they either become serial killers, politicians, people in power, ruthless selfish beings that cause nothing but suffering around them. Some become surgeons and save lives. Some have families. It gets even more complicated but this article is not about psychopathy (most assuredly I could write 100 articles on that topic alone but time is short and ... all that yadda yadda).

So given all this confusion, no wonder people are tired, they want clarity in a simple way. They want to revert to the simple things. They want to shun away all the reality and find a refuge within. Some even wish they could die (without pain or without even knowing) so that it can all end. 

The point of all this was not to inform, but to bring to the table the important reality that we are part of, so that liberation makes sense. You cannot aim for liberation if you don't have a clear picture of what you are seeking liberation from. Liberation from what? From this. From that.

#What is the ego?#


Enlightenment is nothing supernatural, it is simply chemistry in a human brain. LSD could get you there or various breathing yoga exercises, or simply imagining things until you can't tell reality from fantasy and your fantasy is so vivid you call it "heaven". It's all very simple to explain in material terms through science nowadays. We have data, we have knowledge. Mysticism is just that. However, it is the experience that is relevant, that is what matters to you. If you had schizophrenia and severe psychosys and saw things that are as real hallucinations as anything else, no point in knowing it's your brain doing it - the only thing that matters to you is they are real to you. That is what I call "relevance". And don't take this thing lightly, this word is important, it will come up over and over again. 

So mysticism is all about the art of experiencing, the art of being a subject, the science of consciousness and its infinite possibilities.
