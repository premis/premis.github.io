Rethinking the approach to spirituality. What is it really? Is it just a word?

You see, words are meaningless by themselves because meaning is lived and words are just vessels. Scribbles on the walls of caves, flutters of sounds swimming in the air. Movement of hands. If it weren't for a living consciousness, meaning would not exist. 

I call the consciousness - the soul. One word. But it is not the word that makes it be, it is the experience of it that makes it be. Consciousness is awareness. Awareness is experience. Experience is life. 


When people get stuck in words they cannot experience their meaning. Because their consciousness is fixated, their very being is stuck. Their thoughts are stuck in ideas, objects and things. Stuck in places, theories, teachings, and precepts. Ethics, norms, roles and identities, definitions and ratiocinations. Stuck in rituals, in culture, in obsessions. Stuck in rules and habits and memories. Stuck in imaginations, constructions and dreams. Those lucid dreams they beseech, poisoning their blood with neurotransmitters and calling it heaven. They do not experience Existence, they experience themselves and call it God. Words can be realigned to liberate or to enslave. And enslavement has many forms: it is the enslavement of belief, of conceptualization, of understanding, and of experience which is "mine". The ego is Mara - the illusion of existence which is not Existence. The greatest obstacle to the spiritual finality. 

What is then spiritual awakening? 

It came to me just so and this is how I write it: 
> I have seen in my innumerable lives, ever since I have first walked upon the Earth in the form of all
> beings (sarva-bhūta-rūpena), in all humans (manuṣyeṣu) within which I am even now as I speak, the 
> delusion (moham) of those who believe themselves to be animals (paśavaḥ) and nothing more, and then 
> the delusion (moham) of those who believe themselves to be human (mānavaḥ) and nothing less, and then
> the delusion (moham) of those who believe themselves to be gods (devaḥ). I grieve (śocāmi) for them
> all for their deliverance (mokṣa) and that is the mire of samsara (saṃsārasya pān̄ka), the neverending
> (anantata) of the One (ekasya) who does not know Oneself (ātmanam na vetti).



Now goes on a deeper exposition of what the above means (simply to guide the mind to a more conductive state to allow the experience or even invite it sooner):

- What does "the One" mean? It means that the same soul, consciousness, the same is in all life. In me and in you and in everybody, this I can see to be the same fundamental consciousness in all humans. It is "one" consciousness, there are not many. The contents (experiences) thereof differ, be they experiences from the senses, experiences from the mind as thoughts, or from the mind as imagination, from the deeper unconscious mind as dreams and hallucinations, they are all experiences that delineate and construe the localized self and thus give rise to the illusion of self and of "me". In turn this prevents the all-pervading Consciousness from becoming aware in Its entirety. 

- But this is still not the final way it is. The contents of consciousness are the consciousness itself, moving from one human brain to another. The Universe becomes consciousness in human brains, because all particles, waves, fields, string or quarks, are fundamentally consciousness - that which becomes conscious. It does not mean the particle is aware, that it is a subject. Far from it. It means that the particle can be cognized or become conscious qualia. The entire Universe is a large conscious awareness focused in intense beings like animals and human brains. The Universe is the "body" and the humans are the "members". It is the brain that produces the separate, individual "me" or "I". That is "who does not know Oneself" part. 

This was the hard part, because words cannot render it any more clear than this. It follows that a human being is not a god, does not become a god, does not "become" anything that is not already. A human being remains a human being. But one human being who has transcended the limitation of experience, whose self is seen as what it truly is (the temporary delineation of Consciousness) acts no longer in that ignorance. Such a human being is said to be liberated. Liberated from that ignorance and their "way" of being is no longer tainted with all the futility and vanity of their previous condition. 

What is that ignorance? This is hard to write. An ignorant being is not much different than a liberated being, they both have thoughts, feel feelings, there is nothing fundamentally different. After all the only thing that changes is "getting it" nothing more. All else just follows from that. One can say: “The ego is illusion.” and still be completely driven by ego. One can chant "All is One" and still have zero clue to what it means experientially. It's not about getting it intellectually, but getting it experientially. We say they have come to "realize" and they are self-realized. Some say they are illuminated (but more and more I find this term abhorrent, as if illuminated by Lucifer's dark enlightenment or some mystical godification had occured and they became supernatural - all knowing and all powerful). Such is simply naive and childish. There is also the term "awakened" and this one is a bit more close to what it is like. 



But after all this heavylifting, let us drop it to simple sentences - simple works better. It's like you believe in Santa Claus. Then you find out that was a fabrication. Now you are awake. Still you. Truth was there all along, Santa wasn't. It's that simple. 

Same in here, you realize "you" are Him and He was making "you" all along. He is in you, not "you" and you? You don't exist. Your body does, your mind does, all that does, but the consciousness you called "mine" and sense of self, while still there, are not "you" or "yours" because there is no "you" or "yours" they are just abstract mind gimmicks, or better yet... they are just "words".
